What We Offer

Programming formats.

Every format can be customized for your audience size, venue, scheduling constraints, and programming goals.

Format 01

The Core Talk

Our signature speaking event. Personal narrative, research, and audience interaction in a tight, well-paced package. Works in nearly any campus venue. Best for student-wide events, orientation, wellness programming, and campus speaker series. Rooms of 30–600 welcome.

45–60 min

Format 02

The Deep Dive

An extended event with additional time for Q&A, small-group reflection, and facilitated discussion. Best for mental health awareness weeks, counseling center partnerships, residential life programming, and academic department events.

75–90 min

Format 03

Film + Talk

Most Requested

Screen Disconnected, then transition immediately into a live conversation with the filmmakers. The film establishes emotional connection; the talk opens the room. Consistently our highest-rated format. Perfect for campus-wide moments, signature events, and mental health week programming.

2–2.5 hrs

Format 04

Classroom Session

A focused, discussion-based session for academic courses. Designed for communication, psychology, public health, media studies, and social work departments. Includes framing for academic reflection and optional assignments.

50–75 min

Format 05

Multi-Event Day

Multiple sessions across one campus visit — department talks, residence hall events, a faculty discussion, and a campus-wide evening event. Best use of travel for campuses seeking broad reach. Pricing reflects the full-day commitment.

Full Day

All formats include: pre-event consultation, promotional materials, and post-event follow-up resources for students.

Logistics

The practical
details.

We've designed the booking process to be straightforward for programming boards and student life staff with standard campus scheduling workflows.

We're flexible on timing, venue, and format. We're not flexible on quality.

Travel & Availability

We travel nationally. Booking requests are accepted on a rolling basis, with most dates available 4–12 weeks out. Contact us early for high-demand periods like fall orientation and spring mental health awareness weeks.

Tech & Venue Requirements

We require a projector or screen, basic PA/sound system, and a laptop HDMI connection. We've performed in auditoriums, student unions, classrooms, dining halls, and outdoor spaces. Most standard campus venues work.

Pricing

Pricing varies by event format, travel distance, and campus type. We offer flexible rates for community colleges, smaller institutions, and campuses serving underrepresented populations. Contact us for a quote — we make it work.

What's Included

  • Pre-event planning call with your programming contact
  • Digital promotional materials (posters, social graphics)
  • Full event execution — we handle our own logistics
  • Post-event student reflection guide (PDF)
  • Optional: faculty discussion guide for academic follow-up
